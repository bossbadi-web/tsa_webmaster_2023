What is the training process for astronauts?

Astronauts practice in life-sized models, called Space Vehicle Mock-up Facility. This way, they can learn how to move about in the facility.

There are many different ways to train for microgravity:

  • There is a plane called KC-135, which is one way they experience microgravity. They fly the plane all the way into the air and quickly nosedive, providing a microgravity environment.
  • There is a "Precision Air-Bearing Floor," which allows astronauts to experience moving heavy objects as if they are in space, where there is no friction present.
  • To practice leaving the space shuttle to make repairs to the ship, astronauts go underwater. Since waterâ€™s gravitational level is lower than normal, it is a perfect place to practice. Furthermore, astronauts could practice in the Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory Pool, where they can insert panels simulating the outside of space.
People need to be physically fit to be an astronaut. In space, it may be hard to stay fit. Fortunately, there are specialized treadmills in the space station.
