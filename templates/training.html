What is the training process for astronauts?

Astronauts practice in life-sized models, called Space Vehicle Mock-up Facility. This way they get to learn how to move about in the facility.

There are many different ways to train for microgravity:

  • There is a plane called KC-135 which is one way they experience microgravity, they fly the plane all the way into the air, and then quickly nosedive providing a microgravity environment.
  • There is a “Precision Air-Bearing Floor” that allows astronauts to experience moving huge objects as if they are in space, where there is no friction
  • To practice leaving the space shuttle to make repairs to the ship, they go underwater. Since water’s gravitational level is lower than normal, it is a perfect place to practice. And astronauts could practice in the Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory Pool, where they can insert panels simulating the outside of the space.
People need to be physically fit to be an astronaut. In space, it may be hard to stay fit, however there are specialized treadmills in the space station
